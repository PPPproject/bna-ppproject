Intro

The Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) had been an important pillar of the federal governmentâ€™s response to the economic shocks of the COVID-19 pandemic. The program provided qualifying small businesses with low interest, forgivable loans intended to help keep workers on payroll. PPP funds were established as part of the $2 trillion CARES Act, passed in [insert].

The program drew criticism for poor rules that allowed large corporations to obtain exorbitant loans while disadvantaging small businesses and businesses owned by women and minorities. The Biden administration announced changes to the program earlier this year to aid small businesses, and in late March extended the deadline to apply to May 31. To date the Small Business Administration (SBA) has approved over $795 billion dollars PPP in loans.

In April, the SBA released detailed data on PPP-approved lending. This dashboard explores the distribution of over a million PPP loans to businesses in California, with summary statistics by county.

Some sectors received more support than others. In California, restaurants received the most PPP funds: over $6.1 billion went to full-service restaurants. (Hover for loan volume by sector. Each color represents an industry and 1G = 1 billion.)
